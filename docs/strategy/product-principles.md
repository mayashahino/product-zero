# Product Principles

These principles guide every product, design, and technical decision in Product Zero.

They are the criteria we use to evaluate every feature, interaction, and piece of content.

---

## 1. Help hiring managers make better decisions.

Every feature should reduce uncertainty and help hiring managers understand how I think, solve problems, and collaborate.

---

## 2. Show thinking, don't claim it.

Don't tell people I'm product-minded.

Design an experience that lets them reach that conclusion on their own.

---

## 3. Evidence over statements.

Every claim should be supported by a real project, artifact, decision, or lesson.

Show the work behind the outcome.

---

## 4. Design is part of the solution.

Design is not decoration.

Visual communication should simplify complexity, create clarity, and make information easier to understand, remember, and act upon.

---

## 5. Every element should be intentional.

Every screen, interaction, animation, and piece of content must have a purpose.

If it doesn't improve understanding, it doesn't belong.

---

## 6. Clarity creates confidence.

The easier it is to understand how I think, the easier it is to make a hiring decision.

Reduce cognitive load.

Highlight what matters.

---

## 7. Make complexity feel simple.

The goal isn't to hide complexity.

The goal is to organize it so people can understand it effortlessly.

## 8.Iteration beats assumption.

The best solutions emerge through continuous refinement, not first attempts.
