# Product Zero — Interaction Philosophy

These principles define how the product behaves, not how it looks. They must hold true across every page, section, and future case study, regardless of specific implementation.

This is a living document — revisit and refine it as the product evolves.

---

## 1. Interaction is evidence, not decoration.

Every interaction must reveal something true about how Maya thinks or solves problems. If removing it loses no information, remove it.

*Test: "What does this prove?" No answer → it's decoration.*

---

## 2. The product adapts to the visitor — not the other way around.

Whatever mode of attention someone brings — a guided deep dive or a ten-second scan — the product meets them there, rather than requiring them to change how they engage to get a premium experience. Guided-vs-fast exploration is one expression of this; matching quality across devices and motion preferences (Principle 8) is another.

Adapting to attention span is not permission to adapt down the evidence: the fast path may compress, but it must still show, not just claim.

---

## 3. The visitor sets the pace, not a timer.

No forced autoplay, no locked durations. Within whatever depth a visitor chooses, motion responds to their attention — it never makes them wait.

---

## 4. Signature motion reveals reasoning, not just outcome.

A before/after contrast shows *that* something changed. Showing the sequence — what got eliminated, weighted, or prioritized along the way — shows *how* it changed. That's the difference between decoration and demonstrated thinking.

This applies to the product's signature/narrative moments; ordinary UI feedback (hover, confirmation, progress) is governed by normal clarity and immediacy, not this principle.

---

## 5. Restraint is a signal of confidence.

When in doubt, remove rather than add. Every additional flourish must survive the question: does this shorten the visitor's path to a conclusion, or just lengthen the viewing time?

---

## 6. The medium is the proof.

The way information is sequenced and structured must itself demonstrate the quality of thinking being claimed. Form and content are making the same argument, not one illustrating the other.

---

## 7. Discovery happens in first person.

The visitor should arrive at conclusions themselves. If a moment needs an explanatory caption to land, the interaction hasn't done its job.

---

## 8. Quality doesn't depend on device or motion preference.

Reduced-motion and mobile are not degraded fallbacks — they're required to deliver the same clarity and conclusion as the full experience.

---

## 9. No moment charges a repeat tax.

Anything that delights once must not become friction on a second visit.
