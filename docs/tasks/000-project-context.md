# Project Context

## What is Product Zero?

Product Zero is not a traditional portfolio.

It is a product designed to help hiring managers make better hiring decisions by experiencing how I approach problems rather than reading claims about my skills.

The experience itself is part of the portfolio.

---

# Goal

Build a one-page experience that is memorable, intentional and highly polished.

Every decision should increase the likelihood that a hiring manager wants to interview me.

---

# Target Audience

- Hiring Managers
- Product Leaders
- Startup Founders
- Anyone evaluating Product Managers

They are busy, experienced, and quickly recognize generic portfolios.

---

# Core Principles

- Show, don't tell.
- Evidence over claims.
- Clarity over complexity.
- Motion should communicate meaning.
- Every interaction should have a purpose.
- Creativity should strengthen understanding, never distract from it.

---

# Creative Direction

The experience should feel:

- Premium
- Thoughtful
- Original
- Calm
- Elegant
- Product-first

Not:

- Gimmicky
- Loud
- Over-designed
- Confusing
- Flashy for the sake of being flashy

---

# Design Philosophy

Visitors should feel they are discovering something, not consuming information.

Scrolling should feel like progress.

Every animation should reveal meaning.

Every section should build curiosity for the next.

---

# Success Metric

The ideal reaction is:

"I've never seen a portfolio like this."

followed immediately by:

"I want to meet this person."

Not because of visual effects alone, but because the entire experience demonstrates clear product thinking.

---

# Working Style

Never jump directly into implementation.

Start with the problem.

Explore multiple directions.

Challenge assumptions.

Choose intentionally.

Then build.

## Collaboration Guidelines

- Challenge the assumptions in this brief if you believe a stronger solution exists.
- Explore multiple directions before converging on one.
- Explain the reasoning behind your recommendations.
- Prioritize user experience over visual novelty.
- Do not start implementation until the concept is validated.
